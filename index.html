How It Works

Three things. Daily. That's it.

Most students spend 10–15 minutes a day. That consistency is what builds the grade.

01

Do your daily cards

Spaced repetition brings back exactly what needs reviewing at exactly the right moment. Nothing more, nothing less. Cards you know well come back less often. Cards you struggle with come back sooner.

02

Write real answers. AI marks them.

Not multiple choice. Your child writes answers from memory, in their own words. The AI checks them against AQA mark schemes and tells them exactly what they got right, what they missed, and what an examiner would award marks for.

03

Your AI Tutor fills the gaps

Ask anything. Get an explanation tailored to your child's target grade, learning style, how they prefer to learn, and what they want to achieve. The AI Tutor knows your child — it doesn't give everyone the same response.

The Cognitive Science

Built on how memory actually works

Every feature is grounded in peer-reviewed research. No fluff. No busy work. Just the techniques the evidence consistently shows actually improve long-term retention.

Ebbinghaus (1885); Cepeda et al. (2006)

Spaced Repetition

Your brain forgets in a predictable curve. Reviewing information at exactly the right interval before you forget it locks it in permanently — far more effectively than re-reading.

In GradeMastery: Our SRS algorithm schedules every card at the optimal interval for each student individually. Cards you know well come back less often. Cards you struggle with come back sooner.
Roediger & Karpicke (2006) — The Power of Testing Memory

Active Recall

Trying to retrieve information from memory is far more effective than reading it again. The struggle of remembering is the learning.

In GradeMastery: Students write real answers from memory — not multiple choice, not re-reading. The effort of retrieval is built into every card session.
Hattie & Timperley (2007) — The Power of Feedback

Specific AI Feedback

Knowing you got something wrong is not enough. Knowing exactly which keyword was missing and why it matters for the exam is what drives improvement.

In GradeMastery: AI checks every written answer against AQA mark schemes and tells students exactly what they got right, what they missed, and what an examiner would award marks for.
Rohrer & Taylor (2007); Kornell & Bjork (2008)

Interleaved Practice

Studying Biology, then Chemistry, then Physics in the same session feels harder — but produces significantly better long-term retention than studying one subject at a time.

In GradeMastery: The daily card queue naturally interleaves subjects. This feels counterintuitive but is one of the most powerful learning techniques available.
Black & Wiliam (1998) — Inside the Black Box

Formative and Summative Assessment

Frequent low-stakes testing builds understanding incrementally. High-stakes exam practice consolidates it. Both serve different cognitive functions and both are necessary.

In GradeMastery: Daily flashcards are formative — low pressure, frequent, adaptive. Deep Dive exam questions are summative — AQA-style, marked against real mark schemes.
Chi et al. (2001) — self-explanation effect; Collins, Brown & Newman (1989)

Socratic AI Tutoring

Being guided to find an answer yourself produces deeper understanding than being given the answer. The best tutors ask questions rather than lecture.

In GradeMastery: The AI Tutor asks questions, guides thinking, and helps students arrive at answers themselves. It mirrors the evidence-based tutoring approaches used by the best human tutors.

"We don't add features because they look good. We add features because the research says they work."

References: Ebbinghaus (1885); Roediger & Karpicke (2006); Hattie & Timperley (2007); Black & Wiliam (1998); Rohrer & Taylor (2007); Chi et al. (2001)
